**Post 2**

the problem with most budgeting apps:

they tell you AFTER you've overspent.

you add up your coffee purchases and think "oh." and then nothing changes because the money is already gone.

---

**Post 3**

budget-first is different.

you decide upfront what you're willing to spend per category.

daily limit for coffee. weekly limit for groceries. monthly for subscriptions.

then every transaction is measured against that limit in real time.

color-coded progress bars show you exactly where you stand:
green = safe zone
yellow = getting close (90%+)
red = over budget

---

**Post 4**

built Xpnsio around this idea.

set a budget per category. track against it. see what's left — not what you've spent.

also — your data belongs to you. Google OAuth sign-in, full account deletion anytime, no ads, no tracking.

also — I used Claude (AI) to help build it. being upfront about that.
not "AI built my app." more like: I set up strict architecture rules, Claude worked within them. the thinking was mine, the typing was faster.
