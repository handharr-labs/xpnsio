I built a personal finance app because I kept asking: "wait, how much did I actually spend this month?"

The answer was always: I don't know.

---

**Tweet 2 — The problem**

Most apps make you track expenses AFTER the fact.

You record every purchase, then at the end of the month you see: "oh no."

That's backward.

---

**Tweet 3 — The insight**

Budget-first is different.

You decide upfront:
• $X for food per week
• $Y for coffee per day
• $Z for subscriptions per month

Then you track against those limits.

You always know what's left — before you spend it.

Category budgets. Period-aware (daily/weekly/monthly). Multi-currency.

Color-coded progress bars: green (safe), yellow (warning at 90%+), red (over budget).

The dashboard answers one question: how much budget do I have left?
